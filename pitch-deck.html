Scaling (Phase 2)

DIY until it works.
Then outsource.

Why DIY first

  • $1,500 budget doesn't cover co-manufacturer setup fees (typically $500–$1,000 setup + MOQ minimums)
  • Test the product with real people before committing to 500 units of something nobody wants
  • Full control over formulation iteration — change the recipe based on feedback fast
  • Learn the process. Be the expert. Then bring someone in.

Phase 2: Co-Manufacturer

  • Rootree (Burlington, ON) — co-packer for trail mix / pre-made fillings. Good for Hacker's Trail format.
  • Hownature (Markham, ON) — mint/capsule. Worth a call for Phase 2 mint pivot post-validation.
  • Herbaland (Richmond, BC) — Canada's largest mint mfg. MOQ 5,000–10,000. Phase 3 scale.

Co-manufacturer triggered when: 100+ units sold, formula locked, $5,000+ available for production run.

Risks

Honest assessment.

Regulatory
Plain food track — no NPN needed. Natural caffeine from matcha/cacao only. No added isolates (L-theanine, caffeine anhydrous, B6). No focus/cognition claims on label. Bilingual EN/FR required. New front-of-pack symbol (Jan 2026) — plan for it.
Scale
DIY works for 200 units. At 500+, you need a co-manufacturer. Real Canadian mint co-man minimums: 1,000–5,000 units at $6K–$15K minimum. Chocolate format stays DIY-friendly longer.
Taste
Matcha and lion's mane can be gritty if not properly ground. Test on real people first. First batch might need recipe iteration — that's normal.
Competition
Alice Mushrooms (NYC) is the closest competitor in dark chocolate format. No one owns this in Canada. Channel (Solana/Superteam events) is the moat.

Scaling (Phase 2)

DIY until it works.
Then outsource.

Why DIY first

  • $1,500 budget doesn't cover co-manufacturer setup fees (typically $500–$1,000 setup + MOQ minimums)
  • Test the product with real people before committing to 500 units of something nobody wants
  • Full control over formulation iteration — change the recipe based on feedback fast
  • Learn the process. Be the expert. Then bring someone in.

Phase 2: Co-Manufacturer

  • Brandable Nutrition (Ontario) — MOQ 250, ~$1.50/serving
  • NutriCorp (Ontario) — MOQ 500, ~$1.75/serving
  • PharmaGene (Quebec) — MOQ 500, functional ingredient experts

Use a co-manufacturer when: you have confirmed demand (100+ units sold at events), you have $2,000+ to spend on a production run, and the formula is locked.

Risks

Honest assessment.

Regulatory
"Focus" claims need to be careful. No "treats" or "cures." Structure/function OK. Lion's mane and L-theanine are food ingredients, not controlled. Keep claims vanilla.
Scale
DIY works for 100 units. At 500+, you need a co-manufacturer. Budget $2,000–$3,000 for first outsourced run.
Taste
Nootropic gummies taste medicinal if you mess up the ratio. Test on real people first. Accept that the first batch might be rough.
Competition
US brands (Genius Brands) could enter Canada. But they're not at Superteam events. Channel is the moat.

## CALL 1 — York Region Public Health (1-800-361-5653)

**Before you call:** Have product description ready. They will ask what you're making.

**Script:**

"Hi, I'm calling to confirm whether a product I'm planning to make would qualify for the low-risk food exemption under Ontario Regulation 493/17.

Here's what I'm making: a pressed mint tablet — essentially a small 1-gram mint, similar to a Tic Tac. It's made from food-grade ingredients including xylitol, cocoa powder, coffee extract, matcha powder, lion's mane mushroom extract, and standard food additives. It's pressed into tablet form using a manual tabletop press, packaged in a 30-tablet flip-top tin, and sold directly to consumers.

My questions:
1. Does this product qualify as a low-risk food under O. Reg. 493/17 for home-based food businesses?
2. Would I need a separate food safety certification, or would the home-based food business registration through ServiceOntario be sufficient?
3. Are there any specific labeling requirements beyond the standard CFIA nutrition facts table?

I've heard the regulation allows home processors to make low-risk foods like jams, pickles, baked goods, and similar items without a full food safety inspection. I'm trying to confirm where a pressed mint tablet falls."

**What they might say:**
- "We don't pre-approve products — you register with ServiceOntario and they determine eligibility"
- "If it's just food ingredients mixed and pressed, it may qualify — but we can't give a definitive answer until you register"
- "You'll need a food handler certificate regardless"

**If they say it's NOT exempt:** Ask what the full approval path would look like and how long it takes. Get the name of the person you spoke with.

## FORM — CIPO Trademark Search (do this yourself, 5 min)

**URL:** ised-isde.canada.ca/cipo

**Steps:**
1. Go to: ised-isde.canada.ca/cipo
2. Click "Search a trademark" (top right)
3. Click "Search" (don't need to log in)
4. In the " Trademark" field, type: **Overclock**
5. In the "Nice Class" field, type: **30** (food/confectionery)
6. Click Search
7. If any results show "Overclock" in Class 30 — STOP, talk to me before proceeding
8. If no results — you're clear, screenshot the results and send to me

**What you're checking:** Whether anyone in Canada has already trademarked "Overclock" for food products (Class 30 includes candy, chocolate, cocoa, coffee, tea, rice, etc. — the broad food category).

**If there's a conflict:** I'll advise on alternatives. Don't register the domain or use the name publicly until we resolve it.

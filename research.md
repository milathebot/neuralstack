## REGULATORY — Canada

### Health Canada / CFIA Requirements

**Functional Food Claims:**
- Conventional snacks with functional ingredients (mushrooms, vitamins, protein) do NOT require NPN (Natural Product Number) if claims are structure/function only and not drug claims
- Cannot say "treats," "cures," "prevents disease" — structure/function OK (e.g., "supports focus," "helps mental clarity")
- Must be substantiable — need a health claim rationale document

**Labeling (CFIA):**
- Bilingual (English + French) — mandatory
- Nutrition Facts table required
- Allergen declarations (top 10) — mandatory
- Ingredient list — descending order by weight
- Net quantity — metric units
- Manufacturer name + address

**Packaging claims to avoid:**
- "Clinically proven" — requires RCT
- "Cures [condition]" — drug claim
- "Treats [condition]" — drug claim
- Specific enzyme or metabolic claims without evidence

**Packaging claims that ARE safe:**
- "Contains [ingredient] for focus"
- "Vitamin B6 contributes to normal cognitive function"
- "Source of protein"
- "Natural source of antioxidants"

**Novel ingredients (e.g., lion's mane, reishi, new protein sources):**
- Must verify if Health Canada considers ingredient "novel" — if yes, requires pre-market approval as novel food
- Lion's mane and reishi are generally accepted as food ingredients (not novel) — safe to use

## MARKET DIFFERENTIATORS (Gaps)

1. **Gamer/coder first positioning** — existing Canadian brands skew gym/general wellness. The gaming niche in Canada is underserved for functional snacks. Strong community tie-in with Solana/Superteam events.

2. **Nootropic-first, not supplement-first** — most competitors lead with protein or general wellness. Leading with cognitive function (focus, flow, mental clarity) for a specific audience (gamers, coders) is a clearer positioning.

3. **Event/channel strategy** — no major functional snack brand is actively using crypto/Web3 event circuits as a primary channel. This is a unique advantage given your network.

4. **Clean label + taste** — most nootropic products taste medicinal. Focus on "actually tastes good" as a primary differentiator. Functional AND delicious.

5. **Co-manufacturer with low MOQ** — ability to test quickly at events without committing to 5,000-unit runs. Speed to market advantage.

---

## RISKS

- **Consumer education required** — nootropic snacks are still a new concept for mainstream. Need to explain what "focus blend" means at point of sale.
- **Regulatory navigation** — structure/function claims require care. Budget for a regulatory review of labels before printing.
- **Co-manufacturer lead times** — 4–8 weeks for first run. Factor into event timing.
- **Pricing pressure** — $5–7 wholesale is tight at 500-unit runs. Scale to 2,000+ units to improve margins meaningfully.
- **US competitors** — Genius Brands and Ka'Chava could enter Canada via Amazon. Not an immediate threat but watch.

# LateNight — Market Research
*Toronto, Canada | Functional dark chocolate & trail mix for gamers/coders/builders*

---

## MARKET CONTEXT

**Category:** Functional snacks / "brain food" for late-night focus
**Audience:** Gamers (18–35), coders (18–40), Web3 builders, 3am grinders
**Format:** Dark chocolate squares (70%+) OR trail mix — 1 pouch = 1 serving
**Price:** $5 wholesale / $8–10 retail
**Venue:** Events (Superteam Canada, Solana meetups, farmers markets, festivals)
**Partnership:** Complementary to drink brand (bundle opportunity, no conflict)
**Note:** Gummies off the table for Phase 1 — Ontario home food regulations and co-manufacturer MOQs make them non-viable at this stage.

---

## PRODUCT FORMAT DECISION

### Chosen: Dark Chocolate Squares OR Trail Mix

**Dark chocolate squares (70%+):**
- 8–10g per pouch
- Clean, minimal ingredient list
- Culinary matcha and lion's mane integrate well into chocolate

**Trail mix:**
- Almonds, cacao nibs, dried berries, matcha-infused components
- Lion's mane powder mixed in
- More mass-market familiar

**Why NOT gummies (Phase 1):**
- Ontario home food regulations prohibit non-canned preserved foods at home
- Co-manufacturer MOQs for gummies start at 1,000–5,000 units ($6K–$15K minimum)
- DIY gummy production is not feasible at current stage

## INGREDIENT STACK (Validated)

### Core Formula

| Ingredient | Amount | Role |
|------------|--------|------|
| Matcha green tea powder | 1.5–2g | Natural caffeine ~50–70mg, L-theanine ~30–60mg, EGCG |
| Dark chocolate 70%+ | 8–10g | Cocoa flavanols, theobromine, natural B vitamins |
| Lion's mane powder (fruiting body) | 500–1000mg | Brand-defining, culinary use, nootropic |
| Sea salt | pinch | Flavor, mineral |
| MCT oil | optional | Clean energy, satiety |

### Natural Caffeine Delivery
Matcha provides **50–70mg natural caffeine** per serving — approximately one coffee's worth. This is delivered via the whole plant matrix (not isolated caffeine), which provides a smoother, more sustained effect alongside the L-theanine naturally present in matcha.

### INGREDIENTS WE ARE NOT USING (and why)

| Excluded Ingredient | Reason |
|---------------------|--------|
| Isolated caffeine powder | Not needed — matcha provides natural caffeine |
| L-theanine powder | Not needed — matcha contains natural L-theanine |
| Vitamin B6 (isolated) | Not needed — dark chocolate provides natural B vitamins |
| Alpha-GPC | Too pharmaceutical, not a food ingredient |
| Bacopa | Not a culinary ingredient, different regulatory track |
| Rhodiola | Not a food ingredient, different regulatory track |
| Ashwagandha | Not a culinary ingredient |
| Huperzine A | Pharmaceutical nootropic, not a food |
| Vinpocetine | Pharmaceutical, not a food |
| DMAE | Not approved as a food ingredient in Canada |
| PEA | Not an approved food ingredient in Canada |

**Philosophy:** Stay in "plain food" territory. Everything used is a recognizable food ingredient. No isolated synthetic compounds. No pharmaceutical nootropics.

## REGULATORY (Canada)

### Framework

**Stay in "plain food" track.** No NPN required. No health claims. All ingredients are conventional foods.

### Allowed

- Natural caffeine from matcha and cacao (no isolated caffeine added)
- Natural L-theanine from matcha (no L-theanine powder added)
- Natural B vitamins from dark chocolate
- Lion's mane as a food ingredient (fruiting body)
- Claims like "made with matcha" and "contains lion's mane"
- Bundle with complementary products

### Not Allowed

- No isolated caffeine or caffeine powder
- No isolated B vitamins (B6, etc.)
- No focus/cognition claims ("improves focus," "enhances memory," etc.)
- No "treats," "cures," "clinically proven" language
- No pharmaceutical nootropics (alpha-GPC, bacopa, rhodiola, ashwagandha, huperzine A, vinpocetine, DMAE, PEA)

### Labeling Requirements

- **Bilingual labels** (English + French required)
- **Nutrition Facts table** mandatory
- **Allergen declarations** (top 10 allergens)
- **Caffeine disclosure** — natural caffeine from matcha/cacao must be declared (50mg = approx one coffee's equivalent)
- **Ingredient list** in descending order by weight
- **Front-of-pack nutrition symbol** — new Canada regulations take effect January 2026 (high-in-sodium, high-in-sugar, high-in-saturated fat symbols required on front of packaged foods)

## DIFFERENTIATORS

3. **Cleanest label in category** — no isolated compounds, no pharmaceutical nootropics, just food

## RISKS

- Consumer education at point of sale (what does this taste like? does it work?)
- DIY production limits scale — max ~200–500 pouches per run at home
- Regulatory risk if any ingredient is reclassified
- No focus/cognition claims = harder to communicate product benefits
- Co-manufacturer route requires significant capital ($6K–$15K) and commitment to 1,000+ units
- New front-of-pack nutrition symbols (Jan 2026) may require reformulation or packaging changes if product is high in sodium, sugar, or saturated fat

## PHASE 1 RECOMMENDATION

1. **Start with chocolate squares** — slightly higher all-in cost but cleaner production, better shelf life, more brandable
2. **DIY production** at home or small kitchen — stay under $1,000 all-in for first 200 pouches
3. **Sell at events** at $8–10 retail — events allow face-to-face education
4. **Target break-even at ~190 pouches sold**
5. **Reinvest profits** into next run; scale to 500 units
6. **Revisit co-manufacturer** only when ready for 1,000+ unit commitment

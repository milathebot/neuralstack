## EXECUTIVE SUMMARY

MunchPad is a functional snack brand targeting gamers, coders, and Web3 builders in Canada. One SKU: dark chocolate squares with matcha and lion's mane mushroom. Made at home under Ontario's low-risk food exemption. Sold direct at events.

No gym references. No wellnessfluencer copy. No "optimize your biohacking." Just: you're in the zone, take one of these.

**Format:** Focus Bites — dark chocolate squares (10g each), 5 per pouch
**Stack:** Matcha + Cacao + Lion's Mane mushroom powder
**Price:** $8–10 retail / $5 wholesale
**Audience:** 18–35, desk-bound 8–16 hours/day, gamers, coders, Web3 builders
**Channel:** Events first — Superteam Canada, Solana meetups, farmers markets, hackathons
**Partnership:** Complementary to drink brand (no conflict, bundle opportunity)
**Budget:** $1,250–$1,725 to first 200 pouches
**Timeline:** 6 weeks to first sale

**Why not gummies:** Ontario home food regulations (O. Reg. 493/17) don't include gummies on the low-risk list — commercial kitchen required. Co-manufacturer MOQs start at 1,000–5,000 units at $6K–15K minimum. Chocolate and trail mix are explicitly on the low-risk list. Diy home production is the path.

## 5. PRODUCT

### Focus Bites — Dark Chocolate Squares

**Description:** 70% dark chocolate with matcha center and lion's mane mushroom powder. Sea salt. 10g per square = one serving.

**What's in it:**
- Matcha green tea powder (1.5–2g) — natural caffeine ~50–70mg + L-theanine ~30–60mg + EGCG
- Dark chocolate 70%+ (8–10g) — cocoa flavanols, theobromine, natural B vitamins, magnesium
- Lion's mane mushroom powder (500–1,000mg fruiting body) — brand-defining functional ingredient
- Sea salt, vanilla, MCT oil (optional)

**What's NOT in it (and why):**
- Isolated caffeine or caffeine anhydrous — triggers supplemented foods framework
- Isolated L-theanine powder — same, regulatory complexity
- B6 or other added vitamins — same
- Alpha-GPC, bacopa, rhodiola, ashwagandha — dose mismatch for snack format, wrong acute/long-term fit
- Huperzine A, vinpocetine, DMAE, PEA — safety/regulatory red flags

**What it does:** Supports sustained focus. Natural caffeine from matcha. Theobromine from cacao provides mild stimulation. No crash, no jitters when dosed correctly.

**What it doesn't do:** Replace a supplement. Claim to be Adderall. Fix your sleep schedule.

### Alternative format: Hacker's Trail (functional trail mix)

**Description:** Cacao nibs + matcha-coated almonds + pumpkin seeds + lion's mane powder + dried tart cherries + coconut chips. 60g pouch.

**Why it exists:** Lower production complexity (no cooking), cheaper per pouch, better shelf stability, easier to scale.

**Trade-off:** Less differentiated sensorially. Crowded shelf. Needs stronger branding to compete.

## 7. REGULATORY

### Path: Plain food (not supplement, not NHP)

Ontario's home food exemption (O. Reg. 493/17) allows production of low-risk foods in a home kitchen. The explicit list includes: baked goods, chocolate, hard candies, fudge, granola, trail mix, nuts, seeds, coffee, tea. Gummies are NOT on this list. Chocolate squares and trail mix ARE.

### What this means for the label

Required (bilingual EN/FR):
1. Common name: "Dark chocolate with matcha green tea and lion's mane mushroom" / "Chocolat noir au thé vert matcha et au champignon hericium"
2. Net quantity: "50 g"
3. Nutrition Facts table — full bilingual format
4. Ingredient list — descending order, allergens identified, both languages
5. Allergen statement: "Contains: ___" (tree nuts from chocolate processing, milk if applicable)
6. Dealer name and address
7. Lot code and best-before date
8. Country of origin

**New (January 2026):** Front-of-pack nutrition symbol if product exceeds sodium/sugars/saturated fat thresholds. Most 70%+ dark chocolate will trigger saturated fat at small serving sizes. Plan for this.

### What NOT to do

- Don't add isolated caffeine, L-theanine powder, or B vitamins — triggers supplemented foods framework
- Don't claim "improves focus," "boosts brain function," "enhances cognition" — triggers NHP classification (NPN costs $5K–$50K, takes 60–210 days)
- Don't use "Focus" in the product name in a way that implies medicinal effect — "Focus Bites" is borderline; "Builder's Bites" or "Hacker's Trail" is safer
- Don't put dose numbers front-of-pack in a way that implies supplement dosing

### What you CAN say

- Describe the ingredients: "made with matcha and lion's mane mushroom"
- Describe general categorical reputation: "matcha and lion's mane have a long history of use in functional eating"
- Call it a snack. Call it chocolate. Call it food.

## 8. PRODUCTION PLAN

### Week 1–2: Setup

- [ ] Confirm domain (MunchPad.ca or similar)
- [ ] Incorporate (Ontario sole prop or LLC — sole prop is free, LLC is $300 via Ontario Business Registry)
- [ ] CIPO trademark search ($50 online)
- [ ] Buy ingredients: Callebaut 70% callets (Costco ~$30/2.5kg), culinary matcha (Bulk Barn ~$15/100g or Jade Leaf bulk), lion's mane powder (Lemon Lily Tea ~$45/500g)
- [ ] Buy equipment: silicone chocolate mold (10-cavity, Amazon ~$20), digital kitchen scale (0.1g precision, ~$30), heat sealer (~$40), mixing bowls

### Week 3–4: First batch

- [ ] Make 50 test squares (one batch = 400g chocolate + 30g matcha + 20g lion's mane)
- [ ] Get feedback from 5–10 people (Discord, friends, Solana chat)
- [ ] Adjust recipe if needed (more matcha? more lion's mane? less salt?)
- [ ] Design label on Canva
- [ ] Print labels on sticker paper (Avery 5163 or VistaPrint)

### Week 4–5: Packaging

- [ ] Source pouches — Amazon CA kraft stand-up pouches (50g size, ~$0.25–$0.40 each)
- [ ] Apply labels
- [ ] Do a smallInstagram/Discord soft launch — 10–20 pouches to friends for feedback
- [ ] Confirm pricing ($9 retail / $5 wholesale)

### Week 5–6: First event

- [ ] Book a table at a Superteam Canada event or Solana meetup
- [ ] Bring 50–100 pouches
- [ ] 15-second pitch: "MunchPad — dark chocolate with matcha and lion's mane. Find your focus. Stay there. $9 a pouch."
- [ ] Collect feedback, DMs, email addresses

### Ongoing: Reinvest loop

- Revenue from events → buy more ingredients → make more pouches → next event
